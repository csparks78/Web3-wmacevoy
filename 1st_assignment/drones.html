What you will find

Here are a few calculators to help you in deciding what motors to use or how to adjust weight to get your quad flying optimally. Sometimes, or more accurately, often its a guessing game on which motor combination to choose to supply enough thrust to lift the craft. Optimally you want to be about 50% on the throttle stick to maintain a hover. Anymore and it takes away from the travel upwards and makes the craft sluggish and could possibly burn a motor or ESC. Lower than 50% is ok, you'll just have a snappier quad.

Thrust Calculator

Enter the thrust output of the motors of your choice and this calculator will tell you the weight the craft will be able to lift. Sample data is from MT2312-960kv motors using 3s lipo at 75% throttle produces 542g thrust.

Example: Thrust = 542g, Motors = 4, Weight (of craft) = 1000g gives a payload weight of 1168g or 1.168Kg

Weight Calculator

Enter the throttle percent (50 recommended), payload weight, number of motors and the weight of the craft to find out the total thrust needed out of the motors for hover at percentage of throttle you select.

Example: Throttle = 50%, Payload weight = 2000g, Motors = 4, Weight of craft = 1500g = 17.5g thrust per motor

Flight Time

For this calculator, you need to know the amp draw of the motors you plan on using (per motor x4) then, enter your mAh for the battery you plan on using. This will give you a rough estimate fo flight time at a hover. Obviously, if you are flying aggressively this flight time goes down.

Example: Battery size (mAh) = 3500, Amps = 20
